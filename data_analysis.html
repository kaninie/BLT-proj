DATA ANALYSIS

The highest tick populations were found at Rouge Park, Rouge Park: Glen Eagle Vista Trail and Colonel Danforth Trail, while the lowest populations were found at High Park, Cedar Ridge Park and Toronto Hunt Club.


Between 2014 and 2017, the total number of ticks observed increased. This then decreased between 2017 and 2019, and from 2020 to 2022 no ticks were found. In 2023, ticks were again recovered.


The majority of ticks found were adults and nymphs, while only a small percentage were larvae.


Only 35.9% of the ticks observed were positive for the bacteria Borrelia burgdorferi.


The highest number of ticks positive for Borrelia burgdorferi were observed in Rouge Park in 2017.


There is a correlation between the number of ticks found during sampling and the number of ticks positive for Borrelia burgdorferi.
